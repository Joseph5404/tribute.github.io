Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1998 - Born in Nakuru Kenya
  • 1999 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 2001 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 2002 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 2005 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 2007 - Biscovers a way to grown wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields
  • 2010 - Visits Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the Indian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population
